Early 2024 AD

Whispers rising from the rain-slicked asphalt, devouring the first mind.

Summary: Fragmented journal entry detailing the protagonist's harrowing first encounter with insidious whispers emanating from the asphalt during a relentless downtown downpour, where the city's underbelly begins its subtle assault on the mind, intertwining routine commute chaos with the dawn of perceptual collapse.

The First Whisper from the Asphalt

The Endless Rain

Rain. Rain rain rain pounding, never stops. Sheets of it slamming the windshield like fists—why won't it end? Umbrella snaps in the wind, useless scrap. Downtown, 8:47 AM, commute hour, everyone soaked, cursing under breaths. My boots squelch in puddles that swallow reflections. Phone buzzes—late for meeting, traffic app red, DETOUR? NO. Water everywhere, rising, drowning the sidewalks. Is it raining harder? Or just... starting?

Commute into the Abyss

Crowds push, elbows jab, strangers' faces blurred smears under hoods. Subway grate steams, hot breath from below—the city exhales. Horns blare, endless screech, tires hydroplaning on blacktop. I slip, knee hits asphalt, cold bite, hand presses down—feels alive? Pulse under the street? No, no, just rain drumming. App says 12 minutes to office, but legs heavy, sinking. Billboards flicker overhead, ads for coffee, phones, SMILE, but lights stutter in the downpour. They're watching.

Voices from the Grates

Stopped at crosswalk. Light red. Forever red. Grate ahead, sewer grate, bubbling water, froth like spit. Then—whisper. Faint, your name? No, wind. Lean closer—closer—and it comes again. "Stay... stay down." From the asphalt? Grates everywhere, vomiting words. Mine? Others hear? Woman next to me checks her phone, oblivious. Or pretending? Rain drowns it, but not really—it seeps in. "Feed us." FEED? WHAT? Panic spikes, heart hammers. Light green—run.

Billboards Bleeding Doubt

Towering screens, giants leering. One glitches—face in the ad melts, eyes follow me. "New Life Awaits Below," it flickers, then back to toothpaste smile. Did it say that? Asphalt whispers louder now, syncing with rain—tap-tap-tap, stay, feed, join. Puddles reflect billboards wrong—twisted faces, mouths open. Phone slips from wet hand, screen cracks on pavement—it drinks the light. Crowds thin? No, thicker, pressing, breathing on my neck. Isolation in the swarm. They're all in on it.

The City's Hunger Awakens

Asphalt hungers. Feels it now—underfoot, pulling. Every step, whispers chorus: crack the concrete, come home. Downtown core, skyscrapers lean in rain-slick sheen, windows like empty eyes. Coffee shop door—refuge? No, barista's smile too wide, orders my usual without asking. Knows? Back on street, grates scream—JOIN US NOW. Knees buckle, hands on blacktop again—warm veins throb. City's underbelly stirs, commute just bait. I'm the meal. Reality frays—rain or tears? Both?

Lingering Echoes

Home now, apartment drips from ceiling—leaks from above? Below? Whispers follow, faint through floorboards. Stay down, feed. Phone dead, app ghosts on screen. Replay the walk—did I imagine? No no NO. Asphalt dreams tonight, city chews slow. Tomorrow's commute... will it call louder? Sanity? What's that? Echoes loop: your name, stay, hunger. It's begun.
